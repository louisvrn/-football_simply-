## 📅 Weekly Workflow (1 Video / 4-6 weeks)

| Day / Block | Task | Duration | Notes / Checklist |
|-------------|------|---------|-----------------|
| Weekday evenings | Research & clip collection | 2×2h | ✅ Collect only what supports your thesis; use templates for charts & diagrams |
| Weekend deep session | Script, storyboard, record | 4–5h | ✅ Bullet script → expand; follow template (Hook → Question → Twist → Mechanisms → Example → Conclusion) |
| Weekend deep session | Edit & visuals | 4–5h | ✅ Reuse visual templates; minimal animations; optional 1–2 short clips |

**Target monthly hours:** ~30–35h

---

## 🎯 Video Template (Reusable for All Topics)

1. **Hook (15–20s)**
   - Provocative / curiosity-driving
   - Example: “xG is everywhere in football analytics — but does it actually measure what you think it does?”

2. **Question / Problem**
   - Frame the video clearly
   - Example: “Can xG tell us anything meaningful about matches or seasons?”

3. **Common Belief / Context**
   - What people usually think
   - Example: “Fans believe xG measures all aspects of a game”

4. **Data Twist / Mechanisms**
   - Highlight contradictions or nuances
   - 2–3 key mechanisms max
   - Example: “xG ignores game state, defensive pressure, and shot context”

5. **Concrete Example / Case Study**
   - Anchor abstract concepts in real matches
   - Use 1–2 examples to illustrate

6. **Interpretation / Insight**
   - Summarize findings
   - Conclude whether metric/phenomenon is useful, misleading, or game-changing

7. **Optional Mini Insight / Closing**
   - Short, shareable final thought

---

## 🔧 Time Optimization & Efficiency Tips
- **Research:** Only collect data supporting the video thesis; avoid tangents  
- **Scripting:** Use the reusable template; bullet points first → expand  
- **Visuals:** Minimalist charts & pitch diagrams; reuse templates; simple animations  
- **Perfectionism:** Done > perfect; focus on clarity  
- **Compounding:** Reuse assets across videos; production time decreases over time  

---

## 💡 Channel Strategy / Positioning
- High-value topics: analytical, slightly contrarian, teachable  
- Differentiation: systems-thinking storytelling, not news/reaction content  
- Content mix: 50% contrarian hooks, 30% system explanations, 20% case studies/examples  
- Consistency > perfection: 1 video/month sustainable; iterative improvement  
- First 3–5 videos define channel identity
