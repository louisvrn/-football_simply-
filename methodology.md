## Method for Developing the Channel & Time Optimization

### 1. Weekly Typical Workload (1 video/month)
| Day/Block | Task | Hours |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Weekday evenings (2×2h) | Research, collect clips, organize data | 4 |
| Weekend deep session (1×4–5h) | Scripting, recording, editing, visuals | 4–5 |
| **Total per week** | ~ | 8–9 |
| **Total per month** | ~ | 30–35 |

**Notes:**  
- Maintain 7–8h sleep, gym, Italian lessons, outdoor day, and chore block  
- Focused blocks prevent cognitive switching fatigue  

---

### 2. Time Optimization / Production Efficiency Tactics
- **Research:** target a thesis first; gather only necessary data → halves research time  
- **Scripting:**  
  - Use a fixed template (hook → question → twist → mechanisms → example → conclusion)  
  - Start with bullet points → expand to full script → reduces writing time by ~50%  
- **Visuals / Editing:**  
  - Build minimal visual system: reusable charts, pitch diagrams, text animations  
  - Animate minimally; visuals follow voiceover  
  - Cuts editing time from 10–20h → 5–8h  
- **Perfectionism:**  
  - If it communicates clearly, it’s done  
  - Avoid endlessly tweaking animations or scripts  
- **Phase separation:**  
  - Phase 1 (weekdays): research/idea  
  - Phase 2 (weekend): script/record  
  - Phase 3 (later sessions): edit/polish  

---

### 3. Production Time per Video (Target)
- Research/data prep: 3–5h  
- Script/storyboard: 3h  
- Recording: 1–2h  
- Editing & visuals: 4–5h  
- **Total:** ~12–15h per video  

---

### 4. Channel Development Method
1. **Focus on high-value topics** — analytical, slightly contrarian, teachable  
2. **Positioning:**  
   - Not a generic football channel  
   - Not news/reaction content  
   - *You are the storyteller who explains systems and challenges assumptions using data*  
3. **Content mix:**  
   - 50% contrarian hooks  
   - 30% system explanations  
   - 20% case studies/examples  
4. **Consistency > perfection:**  
   - 1 video/month is sustainable  
   - Prioritize publishing and iterative improvement  
5. **Compounding effect:**  
   - Reuse templates and assets → production time decreases with each video  
   - First 3–5 videos are crucial to define your channel identity
